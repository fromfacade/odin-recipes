Macaroni & Cheese Recipe:

  • Prep Time: 10 mins
  • Cook Time: 15 mins
  • Total Time: 25 Mins
  • Serving Size: 4

Description:

Are you a big fan of Macaroni and Cheese? I just know that you are.

This stovetop mac and cheese uses staple ingrediants and can come together in just a quick 25 minutes.

Ingredients:

  • 1 (8 ounce) box elbow macaroni
  • 1/4 cup butter
  • 1/4 cup all-purpose flour
  • 1/2 teaspoon salt
  • ground black pepper to taste
  • 2 cups milk
  • 2 cups shredded Cheddar cheese

Directions:

Step 1: Boil Macaroni

Don't forget to bring a pot of water to boil. Cook the elbow macaroni until al dente, which is about 8 minutes.

Tip: For a thicker mac and cheese, make sure that you double the amount of macaroni.

Step 2: Make a Roux

While your macaroni is cooking, go ahead and being working on the roux. A roux is a thickening agent made of one part fat and one part flour that makes up the base of this creamy mac and cheese.

In order to make the roux, begin by melting butter in a saucepan over medium heat. Add flour, salt, and pepper and stir until its smooth. Slowly pour in milk and stir until the mixture is smooth and bubbling. Double check that the milk doesn't burn while doing so.

Step 3: Add Cheese and Macaroni

Finally, the most crucial step of the process: Add the Cheese! Slowly stir in Cheddar cheese until it is smooth and melted. We recommend you grate your own cheese because pre-shredded cheese won't incorporate into the mixture as well as block cheese would.

Once the macaroni is finished cooking, drain and stir into cheese sauce until coated.

Can you bake this Mac and Cheese?

This stovetop mac and cheese is entirely about convience. But if somehow you find yourself with some extra time and a desire for bubbly, baked mac and cheese, you can bake this recipe.

To do so, complete the first three steps above, then top with any desired toppings such as bread crumbs, Parmesan, crushed crackers, etc. Bake at 350 degrees F (177 degrees C) for about 15 minutes. Or until gold-brown and bubbling.
